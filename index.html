Workshop on Foundation Models for Robot Learning in HRI

Conference on Robot Learning 2024

Welcome to the FMs for Robot Learning in HRI workshop at CoRL 2024. Powered by recent advancements in Foundation Models (FMs), robots are now getting close to being ubiquitously deployed in various spaces, including being deployed alongside humans in daily social environments. Therefore, it is crucial to bring humans into the loop and study how to leverage FMs for robot learning in Human-Robot interaction (HRI). As robots become more integrated into our daily lives, we must carefully consider and enhance the social and functional dynamics of human-robot interactions. Foundation models, with their advanced capabilities in natural language processing, machine learning, and human context understanding, can play a critical role in this enhancement. Robots can improve their understanding of human emotions, intentions, and social norms by incorporating FMs into HRI, allowing for more natural and empathetic interactions. Further, these models enable robots to learn from human feedback and experiences, adapting their behaviors to better meet human needs. This allows robots to refine their responses based on subtle human interactions, leading to more personalized and effective assistance. The goal of this workshop is to explore the role of FMs in improving both personalized and long-term robotic learning in HRI scenarios.
