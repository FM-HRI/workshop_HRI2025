Foundation Models for Human-Robot Interaction

Human-Robot Interaction 2025

Powered by recent advancements in Foundation Models (FMs), robots are now getting close to being ubiquitously deployed in various spaces, including being deployed alongside humans in daily social environments. Therefore, it is crucial to bring humans into the loop and study how to leverage FMs for robot learning in Human-Robot Interaction (HRI). As robots become more integrated into our daily lives, we must carefully consider and enhance the social and functional dynamics of human-robot interactions. Foundation models, with their advanced capabilities in natural language processing, machine learning, and human context understanding, can play a critical role in this enhancement. Current robot learning approaches are often task-specific and lack adaptability. Foundation models, like GPT and BERT, have transformed fields such as natural language processing and show promise in robotic manipulation. These models help robots perform complex tasks with greater adaptability by leveraging large-scale datasets and multiple data modalities. Despite advancements, integrating foundation models in HRI is still in its early stages. This workshop aims to explore how foundation models can enable robots to better understand, anticipate, and collaborate with humans across various contexts, and where they fall short. By bringing together researchers from machine learning, robotics, dialogue systems, and HRI, this workshop seeks to push the boundaries of robot learning and HRI. The key topics include the integration of language, vision, and sensory inputs, as well as robotic personalization and continual learning. We believe this interdisciplinary dialogue will lay the groundwork for developing adaptable and safe AI systems capable of transforming the future of human-robot interaction.

Key Topics of Interest

The key topics of interest for this workshop center around the use of FMs in HRI, including but not limited to the following:

  • Foundation models for HRI
  • Data collection for FMs for HRI
  • Generalizability challenges for large data in human-robot interaction
  • Foundation models for long-term robot learning
  • Foundation model for transfer learning in HRI
  • Personalization using foundation models
  • Robot adaptation in HRI using foundation models

Research Questions

The main research questions aimed to be discussed at this workshop are:

  1. RQ1: How can foundation models be adapted to enhance human-robot interaction?
  2. RQ2: What are the key architectural components of a foundation model specifically designed for HRI?
  3. RQ3: What strategies can be used to mitigate the challenges of real-world data collection for HRI, and how can the generalizability of the collected data be ensured across different tasks, environments, and human behaviors?
  4. RQ4: How can robots acquire general-purpose knowledge from human interactions, using datasets that encapsulate diverse human behaviors?
  5. RQ5: How can FMs trained on a large dataset of human-robot interactions be effectively used to tailor robot behavior to individual users with limited interaction?
  6. RQ6: How to tailor FMs to specific HRI tasks in order to facilitate efficient long-term learning from diverse human interactions?
  7. RQ7: Can FMs address data sparsity issues in long-term robot learning, where interactions with individual users are limited?
